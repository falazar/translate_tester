Teacher Lecture: Your First French Words

Alright everyone, now comes the exciting part - we're going to start speaking French! Today we're focusing on the absolute essentials: how to greet someone, how to introduce yourself, and how to ask someone's name. These might seem like simple phrases, but they're the foundation of every conversation you'll ever have in French. Master these, and you'll be able to walk up to any French speaker in the world and start a conversation.

Let's start with greetings. The word Bonjour is probably the most important word you'll learn today. It means "Hello" or "Good day," and you use it any time during daylight hours. In the evening, you switch to Bonsoir - "Good evening." Notice the pattern? Bon means "good," jour means "day," and soir means "evening." French builds words logically like this. Now Salut is informal - use it with friends and people your own age, but never with professors or in professional settings. And when it's time to say goodbye, Au revoir is your formal farewell - literally "until we see each other again."

For introductions, the phrase Je m'appelle literally means "I call myself," but we translate it as "My name is." The beauty of French is that once you learn this pattern, you can introduce yourself anywhere. To ask someone's name, we have two versions: Comment tu t'appelles? for informal situations and Comment vous appelez-vous? for formal. Don't worry about the grammar right now - just focus on the sounds and get comfortable saying these phrases. We'll break down the grammar later. For now, let's practice!

Basic Greetings:

  • Bonjour - Hello / Good morning (formal, daytime)
  • Salut - Hi / Bye (informal)
  • Bonsoir - Good evening
  • Bonne nuit - Good night
  • Au revoir - Goodbye (formal)

Basic Introductions:

  • Je m'appelle... - My name is...
  • Comment tu t'appelles? - What's your name? (informal)
  • Comment vous appelez-vous? - What's your name? (formal)
