Introduction to Subject Pronouns

To talk about people in French, we need to learn the subject pronouns. These are words like "I," "you," "he," "she," "we," and "they." In French, these are:

French English When to use
je I talking about yourself
tu you (informal) talking to a friend, family member, child
il he / it (masculine) talking about a male person or masculine noun
elle she / it (feminine) talking about a female person or feminine noun
nous we talking about yourself and others
vous you (formal/plural) talking to someone formally OR multiple people
ils they (masculine/mixed) talking about multiple males or mixed group
elles they (feminine) talking about multiple females only

Your First Verb: parler (to speak)

Now that we know the pronouns, let's learn our first regular verb: parler (to speak). This is an -ER verb - one of the most common types in French!

French English
Je parle I speak
Tu parles You speak (informal)
Il/Elle parle He/She speaks
Nous parlons We speak
Vous parlez You speak (formal/plural)
Ils/Elles parlent They speak

Examples:

  • Je parle français. - I speak French.
  • Tu parles anglais? - Do you speak English?
  • James parle français et anglais. - James speaks French and English.

Character Story: James's First Day in Paris

20 minutes

📖 A Narrative Adventure with James

We've been following James's story in small pieces all week. Now let's hear his complete first-day experience in Paris! As I read, listen for all the vocabulary and phrases we've learned this week. You'll hear numbers, greetings, questions, and classroom vocabulary throughout the story.

Part 1: Arrival (L'arrivée)

James arrive à Paris! The plane lands at Charles de Gaulle Airport at 9:30 in the morning (neuf heures trente). James is 22 years old (Il a vingt-deux ans) and this is his first time in France. He's very nervous but excited!

At the airport, James practices his French with the customs officer:

Officer: Bonjour. Passeport, s'il vous plaît.

James: Bonjour. Voici mon passeport. (Here is my passport.)

Officer: Vous êtes américain?

James: Oui, je suis américain.

Success! His first real French conversation! James finds the RER train (le RER) to take him into Paris. The train ticket costs 11 euros (onze euros).

Part 2: Meeting Pierre (Pierre, le colocataire)

James arrives at his apartment building at number 25 (vingt-cinq), rue Mouffetard in the 5th arrondissement (le cinquième arrondissement). The building is old and beautiful, with 6 floors (six étages). James's apartment is on the 3rd floor (le troisième étage).

He climbs the stairs (there's no elevator!) and knocks on door number 12 (numéro douze). A young man opens the door. It's Pierre, his new roommate!

Pierre: Salut! Tu es James?

James: Oui! Bonjour! Je m'appelle James.

Pierre: Salut! Je m'appelle Pierre. Enchanté! (Nice to meet you!)

James: Enchanté!

Pierre: Comment ça va? Le voyage, ça va?

James: Ça va bien, merci! Je suis fatigué. (I'm tired.)

Pierre shows James the apartment. It's small but nice! There's a kitchen (une cuisine), a bathroom (une salle de bain), and two bedrooms (deux chambres). Pierre points to things and helps James learn the words:

  • Voici la table (Here is the table)
  • Voici une chaise (Here is a chair)
  • Voici la porte (Here is the door)
  • Voici la fenêtre (Here is the window)

Pierre says: Bienvenue! Welcome! James is happy. Pierre is very nice and speaks slowly so James can understand.

Part 3: Looking for the Bakery (Chercher la boulangerie)

After unpacking, James is hungry. Pierre suggests: Tu veux aller à la boulangerie? (Do you want to go to the bakery?) James says yes!

Pierre gives James directions: La boulangerie est au numéro quarante-trois, rue Mouffetard. (The bakery is at number 43, rue Mouffetard.) That's number 43 - not too far!

But James gets a little lost. He stops to ask an older woman (une dame) for help:

James: Bonjour, Madame. Excusez-moi.

Woman: Bonjour, jeune homme. (Hello, young man.)

James: Où est la boulangerie, s'il vous plaît? (Where is the bakery, please?)

Woman: Ah! La boulangerie est là-bas! (Ah! The bakery is over there!) She points down the street.

James: Merci beaucoup, Madame!

Woman: De rien! Bonne journée! (You're welcome! Have a good day!)

Notice: James used vous and Madame because he was speaking to an older woman he didn't know. Polite and formal!

Part 4: At the Bakery (À la boulangerie)

James finally finds the bakery! It smells amazing (Ça sent bon!). There are so many delicious things: bread, croissants, pastries. James sees the prices:

  • Une baguette: 1 euro (1 euro)
  • Un croissant: 1,50 euros (one-fifty)
  • Un pain au chocolat: 1,80 euros (one-eighty)

The baker (le boulanger) greets James:

Baker: Bonjour! Vous désirez? (What would you like?)

James: Bonjour! Je voudrais une baguette et deux croissants, s'il vous plaît. (I would like a baguette and two croissants, please.)

Baker: Très bien! Ça fait quatre euros. (Very good! That makes 4 euros.)

James: Voici. (Here you go.) He gives the baker a 5 euro bill (un billet de cinq euros).

Baker: Et voici votre monnaie: un euro. Merci et bonne journée!

James: Merci! Au revoir!

James is so proud! He successfully ordered in French! He walks back to the apartment with his fresh baguette. C'est délicieux!

Part 5: Meeting the Neighbors (Rencontrer les voisins)

That evening, Pierre invites his friends over to meet James. Three people arrive: Sophie, Marie, and Thomas.

Sophie is 21 years old (vingt-et-un ans). She's from Lyon (Elle est de Lyon). She studies literature (Elle étudie la littérature).

Sophie: Salut James! Comment ça va?

James: Salut Sophie! Ça va bien, merci! Et toi?

Sophie: Très bien! Tu es d'où?

James: Je suis de New York.

Sophie: Ah! New York! C'est super! (That's great!)

Marie is 23 years old (vingt-trois ans). She's from Paris (Elle est de Paris). She studies history (Elle étudie l'histoire).

Marie: Enchanté, James! Bienvenue à Paris!

James: Enchantée, Marie! Merci!

Marie: Quel âge as-tu? (How old are you?)

James: J'ai vingt-deux ans. Et toi?

Marie: J'ai vingt-trois ans.

Thomas is 24 years old (vingt-quatre ans). He's from Marseille (Il est de Marseille). He studies engineering (Il étudie l'ingénierie).

Thomas: Salut! Ça va?

James: Salut! Oui, ça va!

Thomas: Tu études quoi? (What do you study?)

James: J'étudie le français et l'histoire. (I study French and history.)

Thomas: Super! Bienvenue!

End of Day One (Fin de la journée)

At the end of his first day in Paris, James reflects on everything that happened. He thinks to himself:

"I arrived at the airport at 9:30 (neuf heures trente). I met Pierre. I went to the bakery at number 43 (quarante-trois). I met Sophie (21 years old), Marie (23 years old), and Thomas (24 years old). I used my French all day! I said bonjour, au revoir, merci, and s'il vous plaît. I'm tired, but I'm happy!"

Pierre asks: Comment ça va, James?

James responds: Ça va très bien! Merci! Bonne nuit!

Pierre says: Bonne nuit! À demain! (Good night! See you tomorrow!)
