Lesson: Tu vs. Vous

15 minutes
Textbook Reference: Pages 4-5 - Vie et culture: Tu et vous

The Important Distinction:

French has two ways to say "you" - and choosing the right one is crucial!

TU (informal) VOUS (formal/plural)
Friends Strangers
Family members Older people you don't know well
Children Professors, teachers
Fellow students (usually) Work colleagues (initially)
Pets! Multiple people (even if they're friends)

üåç Cultural Note: Handshakes & La Bise

Handshake: Used in formal situations, business settings, or when meeting someone for the first time.

La bise: Cheek kisses (usually 2, but varies by region!) used among friends, family, and social acquaintances. You lean in and make a kissing sound near each cheek.

When in doubt? Wait to see what the other person does, or stick with a handshake!

üéµ The French Alphabet Song

Sing along with the class! Use the melody of the English alphabet song, but with French pronunciation.

Letters That Sound Different:

  • E: sounds like "euh" not "ee"
  • G: sounds like "zhay" not "jee"
  • H: sounds like "ahsh" (silent in words!)
  • I: sounds like "ee" not "eye"
  • J: sounds like "zhee"
  • R: the famous French R! (back of throat)
  • W: sounds like "doo-bluh-vay" (double V)
  • Y: sounds like "ee-grek" (Greek i)
