Part 2: Adjective Agreement

25 minutes
Textbook Reference: Page 43-44 - "L'accord des adjectifs"

"Today is super important! We're learning adjective agreement - l'accord des adjectifs. In French, adjectives must agree with the noun in gender AND number. This is different from English!"

Basic Rules:

"The default form is masculine singular. Then you add letters:"

  • Masculine singular: grand (tall) "Mon père est grand."
  • Feminine singular: grande (add -e) "Ma mère est grande."
  • Masculine plural: grands (add -s) "Mes frères sont grands."
  • Feminine plural: grandes (add -es) "Mes sœurs sont grandes."

"Let's practice with different adjectives. Repeat after me:"

  • petit → petite → petits → petites
  • intelligent → intelligente → intelligents → intelligentes
  • content → contente → contents → contentes

Special Patterns:

"Some adjectives have special patterns. Écoutez:"

1. Adjectives ending in -e (no change for feminine):

  • jeune → jeune "Il est jeune. Elle est jeune."
  • sympathique → sympathique
  • optimiste → optimiste
  • égoïste → égoïste

2. Adjectives ending in -eux → -euse:

  • sérieux → sérieuse "Il est sérieux. Elle est sérieuse."
  • généreux → généreuse
  • heureux → heureuse (happy)
  • paresseux → paresseuse (lazy)

3. Adjectives ending in -if → -ive:

  • actif → active "Il est actif. Elle est active."
  • sportif → sportive
  • créatif → créative
  • attentif → attentive

4. Adjectives ending in -ien → -ienne:

  • italien → italienne
  • canadien → canadienne

5. Irregular adjectives (memorize these!):

  • blanc → blanche (white)
  • bon → bonne (good)
  • gros → grosse (big/fat)
  • long → longue (long)
  • gentil → gentille (kind)
  • beau → belle (beautiful/handsome)
  • vieux → vieille (old)
  • nouveau → nouvelle (new)

Adjective Position - BANGS Rule:

"Most adjectives come AFTER the noun in French. BUT - and this is important - some common adjectives come BEFORE the noun. We call this the BANGS rule:"

B - Beauty: beau/belle, joli(e)

  • "un beau garçon" - a handsome boy
  • "une belle fille" - a beautiful girl

A - Age: jeune, vieux/vieille, nouveau/nouvelle

  • "un jeune homme" - a young man
  • "une vieille dame" - an old lady

N - Number: un, deux, trois, plusieurs, quelques

  • "deux enfants" - two children

G - Goodness: bon/bonne, mauvais(e)

  • "un bon étudiant" - a good student
  • "une mauvaise idée" - a bad idea

S - Size: grand(e), petit(e), gros(se)

  • "un grand homme" - a tall man
  • "une petite fille" - a small girl

"All other adjectives come AFTER the noun:"

  • "une voiture rouge" - a red car
  • "un livre intéressant" - an interesting book
  • "des cheveux bruns" - brown hair

Examples:

  • Mon père est grand → Ma mère est grande
  • Il est sérieux → Elle est sérieuse
  • Un beau garçon → Une belle fille
  • Des yeux bleus → Des yeux verts
  • Un homme intelligent → Une femme intelligente

Part 3: Personality Adjectives

20 minutes
Textbook Reference: Page 45 - "La personnalité"

Maintenant, "let's talk about personality - la personnalité! These adjectives describe HOW someone is, not what they look like."

Positive Traits - Les traits positifs:

"Repeat after me. These are compliments!"

  • sympathique (nice) "Ma mère est très sympathique."
  • gentil(le) (kind) "Mon père est gentil."
  • intelligent(e) (intelligent) "Ma sœur est intelligente."
  • amusant(e) (funny) "Mon frère est amusant!"
  • généreux/généreuse (generous) "Mes parents sont généreux."
  • patient(e) (patient) "Mon professeur est patient."
  • optimiste (optimistic) "Je suis optimiste!"
  • sociable (sociable) "Pierre est très sociable."
  • travailleur/travailleuse (hardworking) "Elle est travailleuse."
  • dynamique (energetic) "Lucas est dynamique!"
  • créatif/créative (creative) "Ma sœur est créative."

Negative Traits - Les traits négatifs:

"These are not compliments. Be careful using these!"

  • méchant(e) (mean) "Il n'est pas méchant."
  • égoïste (selfish) "Elle est un peu égoïste."
  • paresseux/paresseuse (lazy) "Mon frère est paresseux!"
  • pessimiste (pessimistic) "Je ne suis pas pessimiste."
  • timide (shy) "Je suis un peu timide."
  • strict(e) (strict) "Mon père est strict."
  • impatient(e) (impatient) "Je suis impatiente."

Describing Personality:

"You can combine adjectives with 'et' (and) or 'mais' (but):"

  • Mon père est travailleur et généreux - My father is hardworking and generous
  • Ma sœur est intelligente et amusante - My sister is intelligent and funny
  • Je suis optimiste mais un peu timide - I am optimistic but a little shy
  • Mon ami est sociable et dynamique - My friend is sociable and energetic

✍️ Activity - Describe Yourself (5 minutes)

"Write 3 adjectives that describe your personality:"

"Je suis ___, ___ et ___."

Examples:

  • "Je suis intelligent, amusant et travailleur."
  • "Je suis optimiste, créative et sociable."
  • "Je suis gentille, patiente mais un peu timide."

Part 4: Cultural Topic: French Families

10 minutes
Textbook Reference: Page 46-47 - "Vie et culture: La famille française"

"Let's talk about families in France and how they compare to American families!"

Discussion Points:

  • Family structure: In France, nuclear families (parents + children) are most common, like in America. But extended family (grandparents, aunts, uncles) remain very important.
  • Sunday family meals (le déjeuner dominical): A big tradition! Many French families gather every Sunday for a large meal together. This can last 2-3 hours! Do you have family meal traditions?
  • Family names: Traditionally, children take their father's last name. But since 2005, parents can choose: father's name, mother's name, or both hyphenated. What's the tradition in your family?
  • Living with parents: In France, young adults often live with their parents longer than in America - through university and even into their 20s. Why? University is close to home, it's economical, and family bonds are strong!
  • "Allocations familiales": The French government gives money to families with children! This helps support families financially. It's part of France's social support system.
  • Family size: Average French family has 1-2 children. Large families (4+ children) are less common now.

💬 Student Discussion (5 minutes)

"How does this compare to your family or American families you know?"

  • Do you have Sunday family meals?
  • Do you plan to live at home during university?
  • What's similar or different about French vs. American families?

Part 5: Pair Activity: Family Interviews

15 minutes

"Now you're going to interview your partner about their family, then present what you learned to the class!"

📋 Instructions:

  • Partner A interviews Partner B (7 min)
  • Then switch: Partner B interviews Partner A (7 min)
  • Take notes en français!

❓ Interview Questions:

  • 1. Tu as des frères et sœurs? Combien? - Do you have siblings? How many?
  • 2. Comment s'appellent les membres de ta famille? - What are your family members' names?
  • 3. Quel âge ont-ils? - How old are they?
  • 4. Comment est ton père physiquement? - What does your father look like?
  • 5. Et sa personnalité? Il est comment? - And his personality? What's he like?
  • 6. Comment est ta mère physiquement et sa personnalité?
  • 7. Tu as une grande famille ou une petite famille?
  • 8. Où habitent tes grands-parents?
