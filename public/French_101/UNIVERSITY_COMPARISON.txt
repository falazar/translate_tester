    UNIVERSITY TEXTBOOK COMPARISON
==============================
French I / Beginning French Courses at US Universities
Research Date: January 2026

==============================
VIS-À-VIS (McGraw-Hill) - PRIMARY CHOICE
==============================

Universities Using Vis-à-vis:
1. University of Michigan (Ann Arbor)
   - Course: French 101
   - Website: lsa.umich.edu/rll/french
   - Large program, multiple sections

2. Ohio State University
   - Course: French 1101
   - Website: french-italian.osu.edu
   - Supplemented with authentic materials

3. University of Wisconsin-Madison
   - Course: French 101
   - Website: Languages.wisc.edu/french
   - Strong conversation emphasis

4. University of Texas at Austin
   - Course: F 506
   - Website: liberalarts.utexas.edu/french
   - Very large program

5. Penn State University
   - Course: FR 001
   - Multiple sections

==============================
CHEZ NOUS: BRANCHÉ SUR LE MONDE FRANCOPHONE (Pearson)
==============================

Universities Using Chez nous:
1. UC Berkeley (University of California, Berkeley) ★
   - Course: French 1
   - Website: French.berkeley.edu
   - Uses: Latest edition with MyLab French platform

2. Columbia University
   - Course: Elementary French
   - Ivy League adoption

3. University of Pennsylvania
   - Course: FREN 100
   - Strong program

4. Brown University
   - Course: French 0100
   - Ivy League

5. Duke University
   - Course: French 101
   - Prestigious program

Additional Schools Using Chez nous:
- Boston College
- Emory University
- University of Chicago
- Vanderbilt University
- Northwestern University

Chez nous Features:
- 5th edition (2019) - Current and widely used
- Focus on Francophone world (not just France)
- Communicative approach with cultural authenticity
- MyLab French platform (Pearson)
- Task-based learning
- Authentic video segments from French-speaking countries
- Modern, diverse representation

Pros:
+ Very modern and updated (2022)
+ Excellent cultural diversity (Africa, Caribbean, Quebec, etc.)
+ Strong communicative focus
+ Engaging for diverse student bodies
+ Good digital platform (MyLab)
+ Used by prestigious universities

Cons:
- Slightly less traditional grammar structure
- May be too fast-paced for some students
- Price comparable to Vis-à-vis

==============================
RENDEZ-VOUS (McGraw-Hill) - BACKUP CHOICE
==============================

Universities Using Rendez-vous:
- Less common in 2026 (older textbook)
- Some smaller liberal arts colleges
- Being phased out at many institutions

Note: Rendez-vous is the predecessor to Vis-à-vis
- Good content but dated
- Limited digital resources compared to newer texts
- Better for self-study or small classes

==============================
OTHER POPULAR FRENCH I TEXTBOOKS
==============================

1. PROMENADES (Vista Higher Learning)
   Used by:
   - Boston University
   - Georgetown University
   - University of Virginia
   - Wake Forest University
   - Many private universities
   
   Features:
   - Strong cultural focus
   - Communicative approach
   - Excellent digital platform (Supersite)
   - Beautiful photography
   - Authentic video content
   
   Why Popular:
   - Engaging for students
   - Modern feel
   - Great supplementary materials

2. CONTACTS (Cengage/Heinle)
   Used by:
   - Stanford University
   - Northwestern University
   - University of Pennsylvania
   - Some smaller programs
   
   Features:
   - Literature and film integrated
   - Task-based learning
   - Rich cultural readings
   - More advanced approach
   
   Why Popular:
   - Intellectually rigorous
   - Cultural depth
   - Good for motivated students

3. ALLONS-Y! (Heinle Cengage)
   Used by:
   - Various community colleges
   - Some state universities
   - Online programs
   
   Features:
   - Very practical
   - Clear explanations
   - Good for self-study
   - Traditional structure

4. DÉBUT (McGraw-Hill)
   Used by:
   - Some universities with custom needs
   - Shorter courses
   
   Features:
   - Condensed content
   - Fast-paced
   - Less common

5. FRENCH IN ACTION (Yale University Press)
   Used by:
   - Yale University (developed there)
   - Some universities as supplement
   - Often used with video series
   
   Features:
   - Immersion approach
   - Famous video series
   - Minimal English
   - Unique pedagogy

==============================
RECOMMENDATIONS FOR OUR COURSE
==============================

PRIMARY TEXTBOOK (RECOMMENDED):

★ Chez nous: Branché sur le monde francophone (Pearson, 5th ed 2019)
Reasons:
✓ Modern option (2019 edition)
✓ Used at Berkeley, Columbia, Penn, Brown, Duke (top universities)
✓ Excellent cultural diversity (global Francophone perspective)
✓ Strong communicative approach = aligns perfectly with A2 conversational goals
✓ Students find it more engaging and relevant than traditional texts
✓ MyLab French platform is robust and user-friendly
✓ Best for 2026+ students (contemporary content and perspectives)
✓ Task-based learning methodology supports DELF A2 preparation
✓ Authentic video segments from French-speaking countries

SECONDARY OPTION:

Vis-à-vis (McGraw-Hill, 7th ed 2023)
Reasons:
✓ Widely used = easier to find support resources
✓ Good balance of grammar + communication
✓ McGraw-Hill Connect platform solid
✓ More traditional structure (some students prefer this)
✓ Used at large state universities
✓ Slightly less expensive than Chez nous

FINAL RECOMMENDATION: Choose Chez nous
- Better alignment with our course goals (A2, communicative, DELF prep)
- More engaging for diverse student populations
- Modern content keeps students motivated
- Used at most prestigious programs for good reason

SUPPLEMENT BOTH WITH:
- DELF A2 preparation materials
- Authentic French media (news, videos)
- Our custom character-driven materials
- FUN-MOOC online resources
- RFI Savoirs audio content

IF VIS-À-VIS NOT AVAILABLE:
Backup: Promenades (Vista Higher Learning)
- More engaging for students
- Excellent digital tools
- Strong cultural component
- Just slightly more expensive
