DELF A2 EXAM STRUCTURE & CONTENT ANALYSIS
==========================================
Source: France Éducation International (CIEP)
Last Updated: 2025 Format

==========================================
DELF A2 EXAM COMPONENTS
==========================================

1. LISTENING COMPREHENSION (Compréhension de l'oral)
   Duration: ~25 minutes
   Points: /25
   
   Tasks:
   - Understanding announcements and instructions
   - Understanding short conversations about daily life
   - Identifying essential information in radio broadcasts/recordings
   - 3-4 listening passages, each played twice
   
   Topics Covered:
   - Shopping, markets, stores
   - Public transportation announcements
   - Weather forecasts
   - Phone conversations
   - Simple interviews
   - Daily routines and activities

2. READING COMPREHENSION (Compréhension des écrits)
   Duration: 30 minutes
   Points: /25
   
   Tasks:
   - Understanding correspondence (emails, letters, postcards)
   - Understanding informational texts (ads, notices, menus, schedules)
   - Reading simple narratives
   - 3-4 written documents
   
   Topics Covered:
   - Personal letters/emails
   - Advertising materials
   - News briefs
   - Instructions and notices
   - Event announcements
   - Restaurant menus and recipes

3. WRITTEN PRODUCTION (Production écrite)
   Duration: 45 minutes
   Points: /25
   
   Tasks:
   - Write 2 short texts:
     a) Describe an event or personal experience (60-80 words)
     b) Write a formal letter or email (60-80 words)
   
   Topics Covered:
   - Invitations, thank you notes
   - Describing past events
   - Making complaints or requests
   - Expressing opinions on familiar topics
   - Narrating experiences

4. ORAL PRODUCTION (Production orale)
   Duration: 6-8 minutes (+ 10 min preparation)
   Points: /25
   
   Three Parts:
   Part 1: Guided interview (1-2 min)
     - Talk about yourself, family, hobbies, work/studies
   
   Part 2: Monologue (2-3 min)
     - Draw a topic card, prepare, then speak about it
     - Topics: daily life, leisure, work, travel, etc.
   
   Part 3: Simulated dialogue (3-4 min)
     - Role-play a real-life situation (shopping, asking for help, making plans)

==========================================
GRAMMAR & STRUCTURES REQUIRED
==========================================

VERB TENSES (MUST KNOW):
✓ Present tense (regular and irregular verbs)
✓ Passé composé (compound past)
✓ Imparfait (imperfect past - at least recognition)
✓ Near future (aller + infinitive)
✓ Simple future (basic forms)
✓ Imperative (commands)
✓ Conditional (basic politeness: je voudrais)

GRAMMAR POINTS:
✓ Articles (definite, indefinite, partitive)
✓ Gender and number agreement
✓ Possessive adjectives and pronouns
✓ Demonstrative adjectives
✓ Question formation (inversion, est-ce que, question words)
✓ Negation (ne...pas, ne...jamais, ne...plus, ne...rien)
✓ Object pronouns (direct and indirect)
✓ Reflexive verbs
✓ Comparatives and superlatives
✓ Prepositions (à, de, en, dans, etc.)
✓ Adverbs of frequency, time, manner
✓ Relative pronouns (qui, que, où - basic)

==========================================

ESSENTIAL TOPICS (A2 Level):
1. Personal identification (name, age, nationality, profession)
2. Family and relationships
3. Housing and living spaces
4. Daily routines and activities
5. Food and dining
6. Shopping and commerce
7. Clothing and fashion
8. Health and body
9. Work and school
10. Leisure and hobbies
11. Travel and transportation
12. Weather and seasons
13. Directions and locations
14. Time expressions
15. Describing people and things
16. Expressing opinions and preferences
17. Making plans and appointments
18. Past experiences and memories

==========================================
FUNCTIONAL LANGUAGE
==========================================

Students Must Be Able To:
✓ Introduce themselves and others
✓ Ask and answer questions about personal details
✓ Describe people, places, and things
✓ Express likes, dislikes, and preferences
✓ Talk about past events and experiences
✓ Make future plans
✓ Give and understand instructions/directions
✓ Make requests politely
✓ Express opinions on familiar topics
✓ Agree and disagree
✓ Apologize and make excuses
✓ Invite and respond to invitations
✓ Order food and drinks
✓ Shop and negotiate prices
✓ Ask for help and information
✓ Describe symptoms and health issues
✓ Make appointments
✓ Handle simple phone conversations

==========================================
SAMPLE EXAM QUESTIONS (Typical)
==========================================

LISTENING SAMPLE:
You hear: "Le train en provenance de Lyon et à destination de Paris partira 
de la voie numéro 3 avec un retard de 10 minutes."

Question: From which platform does the train leave?
a) Platform 1  b) Platform 3  c) Platform 10

READING SAMPLE:
Email text: "Salut Marie, Je t'invite à ma fête d'anniversaire samedi prochain 
à 19h chez moi. On va manger des pizzas et danser. N'oublie pas d'apporter ta musique préférée!"

Question: What should Marie bring?
a) Pizza  b) Music  c) A gift

WRITING SAMPLE PROMPT:
"You recently stayed at a hotel that had problems. Write an email to the hotel 
manager (60-80 words) describing the problems and what you would like them to do."

SPEAKING SAMPLE PROMPTS:
- Describe your ideal vacation
- Talk about your favorite restaurant
- Describe a recent celebration
- Explain your daily routine
- Talk about a memorable trip

==========================================
SCORING CRITERIA
==========================================

LISTENING & READING:
- Objective scoring based on correct answers
- No partial credit

WRITING:
- Content/task completion (can they address the prompt?)
- Vocabulary range and accuracy
- Grammar and syntax
- Text organization and coherence

SPEAKING:
- Ability to interact and communicate
- Vocabulary range
- Grammar and syntax accuracy  
- Pronunciation and fluency
- Cultural appropriateness

PASSING SCORE: 50/100 (must score at least 5/25 in each section)
