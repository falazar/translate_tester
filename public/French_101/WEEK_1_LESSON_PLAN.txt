WEEK 1 DETAILED LESSON PLAN
===========================
French 101 - Introduction Week
Textbook: Chez nous, Chapitre Préliminaire (pp. 2-27)

===========================
WEEK 1 OVERVIEW
===========================
Theme: Greetings, Introductions, and French Basics
Textbook Coverage: Chapitre Préliminaire - Présentons-nous!
Character Introduction: Meet James, arriving in Paris

Grammar Focus:
- Subject pronouns (je, tu, vous)
- Present tense of s'appeler, être
- Basic sentence structure

Vocabulary Theme:
- Greetings (formal and informal)
- Introductions
- Classroom vocabulary
- Numbers 0-100
- French alphabet

Cultural Topic:
- Tu vs. vous distinction
- French greetings (handshake, la bise)
- Francophone world overview

===========================
MONDAY - DAY 1 (First Day of Class)
===========================
Time: 60 minutes

PART 1: DIAGNOSTIC TEST (20 minutes)
- Written placement test to assess incoming knowledge
- Basic questions: alphabet, numbers, simple phrases
- Listening comprehension (if they have any French exposure)
- Results used to gauge class level

PART 2: COURSE INTRODUCTION (20 minutes)
- Welcome and syllabus overview
- Course goals: Reach A2 / DELF A2 preparation
- Grading breakdown explanation
- Introduce MyLab French platform
- Explain James character storyline: "American in Paris"
- Show map of Francophone world

PART 3: FIRST FRENCH LESSON (20 minutes)
Textbook: Page 3-4 - Leçon 1: Je me présente

Content:
- Greetings: Bonjour, Salut, Bonsoir
- Basic introductions: Je m'appelle...
- How are you: Comment ça va? / Comment allez-vous?

Activity:
- Listen to dialogue (textbook p. 4)
- Repeat after instructor
- Practice in pairs: greet neighbor, introduce yourself
- Introduce James: "Je m'appelle James. Je suis américain."

HOMEWORK DUE WEDNESDAY:
- MyLab: Complete Chapter Préliminaire intro exercises
- Read textbook pp. 2-6
- Practice alphabet at home
- Journal: Write 50 words in English about why you're learning French

===========================
TUESDAY - DAY 2
===========================
Time: 60 minutes
Textbook: Pages 4-8

WARM-UP (10 min):
- Review greetings in pairs
- "Bonjour! Comment ça va?" around the room
- Introduce James's arrival: "James arrive à Paris aujourd'hui!"

LESSON: TU VS. VOUS (15 min)
Textbook: Page 4-5 - "Vie et culture: Tu et vous"

Content:
- When to use tu (friends, family, students)
- When to use vous (formal, older people, multiple people)
- Cultural note: Handshakes and la bise

Activity:
- Role-play: Greeting professor (vous) vs. classmate (tu)
- James meets his roommate Pierre (tu) vs. Madame Dubois (vous)

LESSON: BASIC RESPONSES (15 min)
Textbook: Page 4 - "Pour saluer et répondre"

Teach responses:
- Très bien, merci
- Ça va
- Pas mal
- Ça ne va pas

Activity:
- Chain drill: Students ask "Comment ça va?" and respond
- Add emotion/tone practice

PRONUNCIATION: FRENCH ALPHABET (15 min)
Textbook: Page 8-9 - "Sons et lettres: L'alphabet"

Content:
- Sing/recite alphabet
- Focus on challenging letters: H, J, R, W, Y
- Accent marks: é, è, ê, ç, etc.

Activity:
- Spell your name in French
- Spell James, Sophie, Marcel, Chloé, Pierre

CLOSING (5 min):
- Preview Wednesday: Numbers and classroom objects
- Quick vocabulary review

HOMEWORK DUE THURSDAY:
- MyLab: Alphabet exercises
- Practice spelling 10 French names
- Watch: Learn French with Alexa - "French Alphabet" (10 min)

===========================
WEDNESDAY - DAY 3
===========================
Time: 60 minutes
Textbook: Pages 10-14

WARM-UP (10 min):
- Alphabet practice: Spell names
- Mini-dictation: Simple French words
- James story: "James cherche son appartement" (James looks for his apartment)

LESSON: NUMBERS 0-30 (20 min)
Textbook: Page 10-11 - "Points de départ: Les nombres de 0 à 100"

Content:
- Numbers 0-20 (focus first)
- Numbers 21-30
- Pronunciation of numbers

Activity:
- Count around room
- Phone number activity (pairs exchange numbers)
- James's apartment: "numéro 25, rue Mouffetard"

Practice:
- Bingo with French numbers
- Quick math in French: 2 + 3 = ?

LESSON: CLASSROOM OBJECTS (15 min)
Textbook: Page 14-15 - "Dans la salle de classe"

Vocabulary:
- un livre, un cahier, un stylo, une porte, une fenêtre
- un étudiant, une étudiante, un professeur
- Use visuals/realia

Activity:
- Point and name objects
- "Qu'est-ce que c'est?" game
- James's backpack: What does he have?

LESSON: CLASSROOM COMMANDS (10 min)
Textbook: Page 16 - "Expressions utiles"

Commands:
- Écoutez, Répétez, Lisez, Écrivez
- Ouvrez votre livre, Fermez le cahier
- Levez la main

Activity:
- Simon Says in French
- TPR (Total Physical Response) practice

CLOSING (5 min):
- Quick review: Greet, count to 10, name 3 objects
- Preview Thursday

HOMEWORK DUE FRIDAY:
- MyLab: Numbers 0-30 practice
- Practice classroom vocabulary
- Count to 30 aloud 3 times
- Watch: Easy French Episode - "Counting in French" (8 min)

===========================
THURSDAY - DAY 4
===========================
Time: 60 minutes
Textbook: Pages 11-13, 18-20

WARM-UP (10 min):
- Numbers review: Rapid counting
- Classroom objects: Quick quiz (point and name)
- James arrives at university: "James va à l'université"

LESSON: NUMBERS 31-100 (15 min)
Textbook: Page 11-12

Content:
- Pattern for 30s, 40s, 50s, 60s
- Special attention to 70s, 80s, 90s (French counting system)
- belgique/suisse alternatives (optional cultural note)

Activity:
- Count by 10s
- Age practice: "J'ai ___ ans"
- James is 22: "J'ai vingt-deux ans"

LESSON: ASKING QUESTIONS (15 min)
Textbook: Page 18-19

Questions:
- Comment tu t'appelles? / Comment vous appelez-vous?
- Tu es d'où? / Vous êtes d'où?
- Quel âge as-tu? / Quel âge avez-vous?

Activity:
- Information gap: Find someone who...
- Create character profiles for James's friends
- Sophie: 21 ans, française, de Lyon

CULTURAL COMPONENT (15 min)
Textbook: Page 20-21 - "Le monde francophone"

Content:
- Map of Francophone world
- Countries where French is spoken
- Brief history: Why is French global?

Discussion:
- Watch short video on Francophone diversity
- James's goal: Visit Quebec, Senegal, Morocco
- Students share: Where do you want to go?

CLOSING (5 min):
- Recap week's learning
- Preview Friday quiz
- Any questions?

HOMEWORK DUE MONDAY:
- Study for Quiz 1 (greetings, alphabet, numbers, classroom vocab)
- MyLab: Complete all Chapitre Préliminaire exercises
- Journal Entry #1 (100 words in French): Introduce yourself
  (Je m'appelle..., J'ai... ans, Je suis de..., J'étudie le français parce que...)
- Watch: Learn French with Alexa - "Introducing Yourself in French" (15 min)

===========================
FRIDAY - DAY 5 (QUIZ DAY)
===========================
Time: 60 minutes

WARM-UP (10 min):
- Final review Q&A
- Practice activities: quick oral rounds
- James meets his classmates: Role-play

CULTURAL LESSON (15 min):
Topic: French Greetings in Context

Content:
- Video: Examples of handshakes, la bise
- When to use which greeting
- Regional differences (2, 3, or 4 kisses?)
- Personal space in French culture

Activity:
- Practice appropriate greetings
- Role-play scenarios:
  * Meeting professor on campus
  * Running into friend at café
  * Introducing friend to parents

CHARACTER STORY TIME (20 min):
"James's First Day in Paris" - Reading

Present short narrative:
- James arrives at Charles de Gaulle airport
- Takes RER to Paris
- Meets his roommate Pierre
- Gets lost finding the boulangerie
- Uses his French: Bonjour! Je m'appelle James. Où est...?

Activity:
- Listen to story (read by instructor in simple French)
- Identify vocabulary we've learned
- Answer comprehension questions
- Discuss: What would you do?

QUIZ 1 (15 min)
Content:
Section 1: Listening (5 points)
- Spell words you hear
- Write numbers you hear
- Identify greetings (formal/informal)

Section 2: Vocabulary (5 points)
- Match greetings to responses
- Label classroom objects (picture)
- Write numbers 

Section 3: Cultural (5 points)
- Tu vs. vous scenarios
- Name 3 Francophone countries
- True/false: French greeting customs

Total: 15 points

===========================
INSTRUCTOR NOTES - WEEK 1
===========================

Pacing Tips:
- First day is administrative + diagnostic - don't rush
- Day 2-3: Core vocabulary building
- Day 4: Expand and practice
- Day 5: Cultural + quiz

Engagement Strategies:
- Use James character from Day 1 to create continuity
- Lots of oral practice - they need to speak from Day 1
- Keep it fun: games, movement, music
- Make French "safe" - errors are learning

Differentiation:
- Students with prior French: Challenge with longer dialogues
- True beginners: Extra practice time, peer support
- Create buddy system early

Common Challenges:
- Tu/vous confusion: Use consistent examples
- French R sound: Don't stress yet, model correctly
- 70-90 numbers: Emphasize pattern, practice repeatedly
- Shyness: Start with low-stakes pair work

Success Markers:
By end of Week 1, students should feel:
- Welcome and comfortable
- Able to say basic phrases in French
- Excited about James's story
- Understanding of course structure
- Ready for more!
